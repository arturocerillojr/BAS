What is Budgeting?

Budgeting is the process of planning how to spend your money. It allows you to prioritize spending and balance your income with your expenses.

A budget is a guide that keeps you on the path to reach your financial goals. Budgeting keeps your finances under control, shows when you need to make adjustments to your spending, and helps you decide where your money goes instead of wondering where it all went.

Budgeting Helps You Achieve Academic and Financial Goals

While you’re in college or career school, you’ll need to learn how to manage your finances, plan for changes, and prepare for the unexpected. Budgeting will help you build decision-making skills and reach your financial and academic goals.

Why Budgeting Matters?

  • Helps track your income and expenses
  • Prevents overspending and debt accumulation
  • Promotes savings and financial stability
  • Gives you control over your money

How to Create a Budget

  1. Calculate your total monthly income
  2. List all your expenses
  3. Group expenses by fixed, variable, and discretionary
  4. Set spending limits for each category
  5. Track progress and make adjustments
